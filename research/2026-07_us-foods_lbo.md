# US Foods Holding Corp. (NYSE: USFD) — PE Buyout Memo

**Watch-list buyout · Price $97.27 · Equity ~$21.4B · EV ~$26.5B · EV/EBITDA ~13.6x TTM**
**Base-case LBO at spot: ~10–12% IRR / ~1.6–1.7x MOIC — below hurdle. A 20%+ deal needs a disciplined entry.**
*As of close 2026-07-17. Educational analysis — not investment advice.*

---

## One-line
US Foods is a textbook LBO *business* — recurring foodservice-distribution cash flows, ~1%-of-sales
capex, ~50% FCF conversion, and a defined margin-expansion runway toward Sysco-level profitability — but
it trades at ~13.6x TTM EBITDA within ~7% of an all-time high. **Entry discipline, not business quality,
is what separates a 20%+ deal from a low-double-digit-IRR one.** The judgment call here is knowing when
to pass.

## The business
US Foods is the **#2 U.S. foodservice distributor** (behind Sysco, roughly co-sized with Performance
Food Group), delivering ~$39B/yr of food and supplies to 250,000+ customer locations — independent
restaurants, healthcare, hospitality, education, government. It earns a spread between manufacturers and
operators, enriched by higher-margin private-label "Exclusive Brands" and logistics scale across ~70
distribution centers. Demand is staple-like (people eat away from home across most cycles),
working-capital-light, and low-capex — the profile that underwrites debt.

## Why it screens as a classic LBO
1. **Stable, recurring cash flow** — consumption-driven, low cyclicality.
2. **Low capex (~1.0% of sales) and strong FCF** — FY2025 FCF $959M (~50% of EBITDA) supports heavy debt
   service and rapid deleveraging.
3. **Concrete, self-help value levers** independent of the market: closing the ~150–200bp EBITDA-margin
   gap to Sysco via private-brand penetration, self-distribution/warehouse automation, procurement
   scale, and SG&A discipline. Management's own 2025–2027 plan targets a **~10% adj. EBITDA CAGR** with
   **≥20bp/yr of margin expansion** and a **~20% EPS CAGR**.
4. **Validated playbook** — CD&R and KKR owned US Foods pre-IPO and are **fully exited (by 2023, no
   sponsor today)**; activist Sachem Head's 2022 cooperation agreement installed the current management/
   board. The operational thesis is already proven.
5. **Consolidating market with M&A optionality** — the US Foods/PFG merger talks (terminated Nov 24,
   2025 on antitrust concerns) and the prior blocked Sysco deal show scarcity value; tuck-ins remain a
   lever.

## The LBO — illustrative take-private (~mid-2026, on FY26E EBITDA ~$2.15B)
Modeled in the **LBO / Returns** tab of the accompanying [`modeling/`](../modeling) suite.

| Step | Assumption |
|---|---|
| **Entry** | Offer ~$117/sh (~18% premium) → equity ~$26.1B; + $5.1B net debt → **EV ~$31.2B = ~14.5x FY26E EBITDA** |
| **Financing** | New debt 5.5x = ~$11.8B; sponsor equity ~$20B + ~$0.8B fees (**~63% equity** — a mega club deal, 3–4 sponsors) |
| **Value creation (5y)** | Base 8% EBITDA CAGR (below the 10% plan) → ~$3.16B via ≥20bp/yr margin expansion; capex ~1%; ~50% FCF sweep delevers **5.5x → ~2.0x** (net debt $11.8B → ~$6.4B) |
| **Exit (Yr 5)** | 12.5x (modest compression) × $3.16B = ~$39.5B EV → equity ~$33.1B |
| **Returns** | **Base ~1.6–1.7x MOIC / ~10–12% IRR.** Upside (10% CAGR, flat 14x exit) ~2.1x / 16–18%. Downside (6% CAGR, 11x exit) ~1.2x / 4–6%. |

Two things make this hard, and both trace to the entry multiple:
- **Premium math is unforgiving.** $117 is only ~11–12% above the all-time-high close (~$104). A credible
  take-private headline realistically needs **~$120–130+**, pushing entry to ~15–16x and dropping IRR
  further.
- **The structure is equity-heavy** (~63% equity, ~37% debt) — a direct artifact of a ~14.5x entry not
  supporting more leverage. That is *why* returns are structurally low, not a modeling choice.

## Risks to the deal
- **Entry price is full** — the single biggest risk; ~14–15x caps IRR before operations even start.
- **Thin margins amplify shocks** — at ~4.9% EBITDA margin, food deflation, fuel/labor spikes, or a
  volume downturn swing coverage disproportionately (Q1'26 already flagged elevated fuel).
- **Financeability** — a ~$32B EV take-private would be among the largest LBOs ever; sensitive to credit
  markets and rates on ~$12B of new debt.
- **Exit-multiple compression** — buying at ~14x and exiting below ~12x erases most of the return;
  distributors historically trade in a 9–13x band.

## Recommendation
**Watch-list buyout — underwrite on weakness, not at the high.** This is a high-quality, PE-ready
business whose returns don't clear a 20% hurdle at ~$97. It becomes a genuine 20%+ deal on a disciplined
entry — a pullback toward **~$80–85 / ~11–12x** (roughly where the late-2025 accelerated buyback was
struck) — or on flawless execution of the 10% EBITDA algorithm with no exit-multiple compression. The
right answer at today's price is patience.
