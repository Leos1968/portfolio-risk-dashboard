# Visa Inc. (NYSE: V) — Long / Buy

**Price $358.56 · Market cap ~$675B · EV ~$686B · 12-month target ~$420 (+17%; +18% total return)**
*As of close 2026-07-17. Educational analysis — not investment advice.*

---

## Thesis
Visa is the cheaper half of a global-payments duopoly — a ~50%-net-margin, ~$21.6B-free-cash-flow
toll road on the multi-decade shift from cash to digital — and it is on sale. At ~25.8x NTM and ~24x
FY27E adjusted EPS it trades roughly **20% below its own 10-year average multiple (~33x)** because the
market is over-discounting stablecoin/regulatory disruption. I think that fear is largely priced in,
while the compounding is not: low-teens revenue growth plus a ~$33B buyback compounds mid-teens EPS,
and a genuine catalyst — the merchant-interchange settlement — is de-risking as we speak.

## The business
Visa runs VisaNet, the network that authorizes and settles card payments for ~4.8B credentials and
150M+ merchant locations across 200+ countries. It takes no credit risk; it earns a small toll on
payments volume, higher-margin **cross-border** fees (its best revenue, tied to travel and
e-commerce), and a fast-growing **value-added-services** layer (fraud, tokenization, consulting) that
is now ~30% of revenue and growing ~27%. Three secular engines: consumer cash displacement, new flows
(Visa Direct, B2B — a ~$200T TAM), and VAS.

## Why it's mispriced, not just good
"Great company" isn't a thesis — *valuation* is. Unlike Costco (~42x) or even Mastercard (~27x), Visa
can be underwritten on price today. It has **de-rated more than Mastercard on stablecoin fear** despite
being 2x+ larger in volume with higher absolute FCF (~$21.6B vs ~$14B), giving a cheaper entry into the
same toll road.

## Catalysts
1. **Interchange settlement de-risking.** EDNY Judge Cogan granted *preliminary* approval on June 9,
   2026 (avg U.S. credit interchange −10bps for 5 years, plus a 1.25% cap on standard consumer-credit
   posted rates; ~$38B of estimated merchant savings). Final approval is expected late-2026/early-2027;
   large merchants (Walmart) are objecting, so treat it as a **catalyst-in-progress** — but it lifts a
   multi-year overhang and blunts the Credit Card Competition Act.
2. **Mix shift away from consumer-credit interchange.** Visa Direct (12.6B txns, +27%) and VAS (~30% of
   revenue, +27%) diversify the model against exactly the risk the bears fear.
3. **The EPS engine.** ~$33B of buyback capacity and a 14% dividend raise compound mid-teens EPS even on
   low-teens revenue.
4. **Re-rating** back toward the ~33x historical average as stablecoin fears prove overblown and Visa
   monetizes stablecoin settlement / agentic-commerce rather than being bypassed.

## Valuation
- **DCF:** base FY26E FCF ~$23.5B, ~11% five-year FCF CAGR fading to ~7%, 3.5% terminal, 8.5% WACC →
  ~$395–400/share (≈ $380 at 8.75% WACC, ≈ $430 at 8.25%).
- **Comps:** ~28x (below Visa's ~33x average, in line with Mastercard ~27x) on FY27E adj EPS of $14.90 →
  ~$417.
- The two triangulate to a **base ~$420 target (+17%)**.

**Bull ~$450** (30x FY27E EPS) on accelerating new-flows/VAS. *A $470 / +31% case is defensible but
requires either ~31.5x or above-consensus FY27E EPS of ~$15.7 — I flag it rather than lean on it.*
**Bear ~$300 (−16%)**: multiple compresses to ~20–21x if stablecoin/CCCA disruption bites and
cross-border decelerates. Skew is favorable: ~+17% to +25% up vs ~−16% down on a durable, low-risk
compounder.

## What would break the thesis (risks)
- **Stablecoin disintermediation** — the GENIUS Act enables non-bank issuers; Walmart/Amazon exploring
  proprietary coins that could, over time, bypass card rails and pressure high-margin cross-border. This
  is the central bear case and the reason for the de-rating.
- **Regulation / pricing** — CCCA routing mandates, the settlement's rate caps and surcharging, global
  fee scrutiny.
- **Thin margin of safety** — ~26x forward / ~1.9 PEG; any growth wobble invites compression.
- **Macro** — a consumer or cross-border/travel shock hits the most profitable line.
- **Near-term event risk:** fiscal Q3 2026 prints **July 28, 2026** — nine trading days after this
  memo's as-of date. Size the entry accordingly.

## Recommendation
**Long / Buy.** A wide-moat compounder at a ~20% discount to its own history, with a de-risking legal
overhang and a mid-teens EPS algorithm intact. Base case ~+17% over twelve months with favorable
asymmetry.
